# Community Outreach Strategy

To acquire your first 7–10 customers without cold-email spam, focus on industry communities where appraisers and inspectors already gather.

## Targeted Communities

### AppraisersForum.com
- **Description**: Premier online community for real estate appraisers
- **Best For**: Discussing continuing education courses and sharing marketing tips
- **Post In**: Marketing or Appraisal Education sections

### InterNACHI Forum
- **Description**: Large forum for home inspectors with categories on inspection, education, and vendor services
- **Best For**: Connecting with inspectors who need compliance solutions
- **Post In**: Vendors section or relevant education topic

### BiggerPockets Appraiser Subforum
- **Description**: Part of the BiggerPockets real-estate forums
- **Best For**: Business operations and compliance discussions
- **Access**: Free with basic account

### State Association Forums
- **Description**: Local forums for appraisers or inspectors hosted by professional associations
- **Best For**: Reaching local professionals in your state
- **Action**: Check your state's professional association website

## Posting Strategy

1. **Choose the Right Category**: Respect forum guidelines about where vendors can post
2. **Be Genuine**: Share your personal experience with the problem
3. **Be Transparent**: Clearly state that you built the tool and link to your site
4. **Engage**: Answer questions about licensing and features promptly
5. **Follow Rules**: Read each community's vendor guidelines before posting

## Consistency Without Spam

- Create a content calendar (e.g., post in 2 communities per week)
- Rotate between forums to avoid spam
- Set phone reminders for outreach tasks
- Track posts in a spreadsheet
- Use email follow-ups to keep leads warm

This keeps your product visible without exhausting yourself or annoying community members.
